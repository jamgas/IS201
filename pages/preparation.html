Preparing
for a PhD

Timeline


The following timeline includes the basic steps necessary to prepare for a top-level doctorate program!

*This timeline assumes you have been accepted into the BSIS program.

Year 1

  • Become a research assistant, publish, and come up with your own ideas too.
  • Get admitted to the MISM! Be successful, helpful, kind, and high-yield/low-maintenance.

Year 2

  • Fall: Take MBA 593R-002: Intro to PhD-level Research in Business -See additional class information below
  • Winter: Take IS 571: Introduction to Academic Research in Information Systems
  • Submit article to ICIS, HICSS, or AMCIS with your advisor
  • You (and your spouse, if applicable) have lunch with Dr. Gaskin and his spouse. Come prepared to ask lots of questions

Summer Before MISM

  • Study for and take the GMAT.
  • With your advisor and/or Dr. Gaskin, research the schools and scholars that most interest you. Narrow it down to five or so; start with this list.
  • Teach IS 110, 115, or 201 if an option (ask Dr. Gaskin)

Year 3

  • Meet doctoral program directors for your top five choices at ICIS, HICSS, or AMCIS
  • Nov-Dec: apply to top five choices
  • Jan-April: fly out to universities that invite you, accept one program; gracefully decline all others.

Start your PhD!

April: Graduate with MISM
Summer: Move to new home
Fall: Start the PhD

Opportunities

Mentored Research
  • Take MSB/IS 490R (Mentored Research) to do research with a professor (flexible credits between 0.5 and 3.0)
  • Become a research assistant to get paid (or get credit) to do research. Apply here!
  • Design and conduct experiments and lab studies, collect and analyze data, write scientific articles, and present them at conferences around the world.
  • Become well-acquainted with a professor and her/his network. This will help better position you once you are ready to get a PhD.
Travel and Presentations
  • If fortunate enough to have your scientific work accepted at a scientific conference, perhaps get the opportunity to travel to and present at the conference.
  • Conferences are located around the world and are held throughout the year. Recent years have seen students travel to Dublin, Milan, Puerto Rico, Seoul, Shanghai, Munich, and all over the United States.
Cool Science
  • We study all sorts of things, like human-computer/robot/AI interactions, augmented reality, data security and privacy, augmented and virtual reality, gamification, deception detection, neuroscience, and much more.
Teaching Experience
  • If the opportunity arises, you may get to teach a class (IS 201, 110, 115, 120).
