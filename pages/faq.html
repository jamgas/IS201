Taking the GMAT is an important step in preparing to apply for a doctoral program. You should take practice exams just to familiarize yourself with the format/structure of questions. Make sure to take whole practice tests in a single sitting so that you can simulate the exam experience accurately.

GMAT Scoring Key

  • < 600: Discard
  • < 650: Severely limits your choices, below BYU’s reputation; probably discard
  • > 650: We can work with this as long as you have lots of other great qualities and a great recommendation from a professor
  • > 690: You’re pretty smart and can go nearly anywhere (for BYU, this is probably just over an average around 670)
  • > 720: You can go anywhere and your score sells you just as much as anything else
  • > 750: You probably tried too hard or you’re crazy smart, so you had better have a good personality!

Intense, smart work, including work on research that your advisor finds fascinating. Playing video games all day is not recommended. Use good time management! Find someone to be accountable to, like your spouse, children, or best friend. Work on topics that your research advisor finds fascinating. Working on your own topics is interesting, but not the quickest way to finish. If your advisor is interested in your research, and if you are accountable and manage your time well, you will finish much faster.

BYU IS PhD Prep students are among the very top when it comes to their ranking amongst peers in terms of PhD placement and job placement. Acceptance rates in doctoral programs are about 2-3%, e.g., 3 admitted from 100 applicants. BYU PhD Prep students are placed on the top of the pile.
